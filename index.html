Cat Myths

The truth behind all felines

Unfortunately, there is a lot of misinformation about my furry friends. Here are just some common misconceptions about us that need some serious myth busting.

Do cats drink milk?

View Info

Do cats drink milk?

Most cats are lactose intolerant. It does not sit well with their digestive system. People tend to believe it is part of their nutritional diet, however, it does not align with their digestive system. We suggest you let them stick to water instead.

Do all cats hate water?

View Info

Do all cats hate water?

Most cats are terrified of water. Sometimes this could be due to training with sprays. Scientists believe since their coats don't dry quickly it can make them feel cold and uncomfortable. There are some felines that just love to jump right in the water. You can totally Google it!

Are black cats bad luck?

View Info

Are black cats bad luck?

Contrary to popular belief, black cats are far from bad luck. Like all other friendly felines, they will love you just as much. In Japan, black cats are reveired and considered good luck. They were also worshipped by ancient Egyptians. Black cats face this unfortunate discrimination which leads to a low rate of adoptions and mistreatment from the public. They are just as lovable as any other cat breed or color.

Should Moms-to-be avoid cats?

View More

Should Moms-to-be avoid cats?

Sadly, hundreds of cats are turned in to animal shelters or placed in different homes every year because of this myth. While cats can be infected with a disease called toxoplasmosis, which occasionally can be spread to humans through cat litter boxes and cause serious problems in unborn babies. However, these problems can be controlled, if the expectant mother avoids contact with the litter box and assigns daily cleaning to a friend or other family member.
